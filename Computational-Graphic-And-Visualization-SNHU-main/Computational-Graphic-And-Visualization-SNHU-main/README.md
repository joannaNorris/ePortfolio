# Computational-Graphic-And-Visualization-SNHU
CS330 Computational Graphics and Visualization at SHNU using OpenGL.

This repository contains my final 3D Nightstand Scene project and its accompanying Design Desicions Document. This scene demonstrates my ability to design, model, and render a fully realized 3D environment using computation graphics techniques. The project reflects both technical implementation and thoughtful design planning.

I approach software design by identifying the goal of the project and breaking it into manageable components. Breaking complex systems into smaller, manageable components and iterating gradually is a strategy that can be used in all areas of computer science for both efficiency and quality. For this scene, I focused on defining the onjects (nightstand, lamp, alarm clock, and books), determining how they would be modeled using basic geometric shapes, and planning how to realistically position them. This project strengthened my ability to think spatially in three dimensions. I developed skills in object composition, scaling, and transformations. This project also greatly improved my understanding of lighting and perspective and their importance to a rendered scene's realism. To make this scene appear the most realistic, I started with the most important part of the scene, the nightstand. I worked from the bottom up with every item, and I placed every basic shape in their appropriate spot in relation to the other shapes and items. While working on each item of the scene, I applied all of their colors, properties, and textures before moving on to the next item. Lots of testing and revising allowed the scene to become the most realistic that I could make it.

When developing programs, I focus on building functionality in stages by starting with the core features. With much testing, I am able to expand and refine without breaking existing functionality. In this project, I strengthened my use of modular functions for drawing and transformations. Every individual shape included everything needed within its function to display properly and accurately. Iteration was critical to this project. Nearly every object required resizing, repositioning, and rotation adjustments before they appeared correct in the scene. Each milestone helped me refine the visual quality and accuracy of this environment. In the beginning of the course, I focused primarily on getting the shapes to render correctly. By the final project, my concerns were more about composition, realism, and user perspective. In the end, my workflow was more organized and intentional.

Computer science strengthens me with problem-solving skills, logical reasoning, and the ability to build complex systems from simple components. These skills are valuable across many domains, especially those technical and creative. Computational graphics strengthen my understanding of spatial reasoning and algorithmic thinking. These skills will support advanced coursework in simulation, game development, and interactive systems. This class has made me reconsider my professional goals. Working with UI/UX visualization, game design, or something like simulation engineering now sound of the most interest to me. This project is my favorite thing I have made throughout my whole college career, and I have a deep desire to learn more and create more intuitive, engaging digital experiences.
